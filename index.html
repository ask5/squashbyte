Squash Byte

Squash Byte is a fun game that involves squashing 8 bit binary numbers AKA bytes using decimals.

The game starts with a 8 x ∞ grid of balls moving continuously from top to bottom on a canvas. Every row in the grid represents a random byte with value between 1 through 255. Each ball is a bit having value of 0 or 1 and is black or white in color respectively.

The game ends if the grid touches the bottom of the canvas. Your goal is to keep it from touching the bottom by deleting rows or in other words squashing bytes. The grid shifts upward as you squash bytes.

To squash a byte you need to enter the equivalent decimal number using the keypad on the screen or your computer keyboard. For each squashed byte you get points equal to it's decimal value. Bigger the value of byte, bigger is the reward!

Wrong entry is counted as foul and will cost you 5 points.

Lastly, the speed of the grid increases with your score so keep up the eye-brain-finger coordination to stay in the game!
